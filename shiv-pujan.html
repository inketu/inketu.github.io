üî± What is Shiv Pujan?

Shiv Pujan is the sacred worship of Lord Shiva, the destroyer and transformer among the Holy Trinity (Trimurti) in Hinduism. Devotees perform this puja to seek blessings for peace, health, protection, and spiritual growth. Shiv Pujan can be done daily or on special days like Maha Shivratri, Sawan Somvar, or Pradosh Vrat.

üî± What is Shiv Pujan?

Daily Shiv Puja (Nitya Puja)

  • Simple offering of water, milk, bilva leaves, and chanting of Om Namah Shivaya.
  • Can be done at home before a Shivling or Shiva idol.

Rudrabhishek Puja

  • Powerful and elaborate form of worship.
  • Involves chanting of Rudra Mantras from the Yajurveda while offering various items like:
    • Milk
    • Honey
    • Sugar
    • Ghee
    • Gangajal

Maha Shivratri Puja

  • Annual festival celebrating the cosmic dance (Tandava) of Lord Shiva.
  • Includes night-long vigil, fast, and four rounds of Abhishek.

Sawan Somvar Puja

  • Observed on Mondays of the Shravan month (July‚ÄìAugust).
  • Devotees fast and offer prayers at Shiva temples.

Pradosh Vrat Shiv Puja

  • Done on the 13th day of both lunar fortnights (Trayodashi).
  • Especially effective when it falls on a Monday (Som Pradosh).
